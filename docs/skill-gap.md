# Skill Gap Assessment

If you are starting fresh, these are the skills most likely to block a good Roblox game.

## Critical Gaps

| Skill | Priority | Why It Matters | Good Enough For Alpha |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Luau and Roblox services | Critical | You need server/client separation, remotes, persistence, and performance discipline. | Build one clean gameplay loop without spaghetti scripts. |
| Scope control and game design | Critical | Most first games fail by trying to be too big. | Keep the first version to one map and one loop. |
| Mobile-first UI and onboarding | Critical | Roblox traffic is heavily mobile and drop-off is fast. | Players understand the loop in the first minute. |
| 3D building and environment readability | High | If the map is confusing, retention dies. | One attractive camp and one readable play space. |
| Live-ops and retention thinking | High | Roblox rewards games that update and keep players returning. | Weekly content beats slow perfectionism. |
| Thumbnail, icon, and discovery packaging | High | A good game with weak packaging still dies. | Ship a clean icon, thumbnail, and clear title. |
| Safety and moderation | High | You are building for kids. Unsafe systems create platform risk fast. | Restrict text surfaces and keep interactions controlled. |

## Helpful But Outsourceable Early

| Skill | Priority | Why It Matters | Practical Shortcut |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Animation and VFX | Medium | Adds polish and clarity during tense moments. | Use simple effects first and upgrade later. |
| Audio design | Medium | Sound strongly improves tension and rewards. | Buy a small licensed pack or use simple cues. |
| QA and playtest ops | Medium | Fresh eyes catch confusion and balance issues early. | Test with 5-10 players twice a week. |
| Economy tuning | Medium | Overpriced items or dead rewards hurt retention. | Start with one currency and one cosmetic sink. |

## Highest-Risk Missing Skills For You

Based on the way you asked, the likely missing skills are:

1. Roblox-native scoping
2. Luau gameplay scripting
3. mobile-first UX
4. live-ops packaging and discovery

Those are more important than advanced graphics.

## What You Need To Learn First

1. Rojo, Roblox Studio, and basic Luau module structure
2. RemoteEvents and client/server boundaries
3. DataStoreService for persistence
4. basic combat or hazard loops
5. thumbnails, naming, and first-session retention

## Suggested Staffing

- Solo founder: learn Luau, scoping, and Roblox UX yourself; outsource thumbnail art if needed.
- Two-person team: one gameplay scripter/designer and one builder/UI generalist.
- Three-person team: add a dedicated artist or live-ops/content person.
